The Chicago Bears stun rivals, Green Bay Packers, in a historic comeback win

By Milan Afshar

The Chicago Bears made history on Saturday with a comeback playoff win over the Green Bay Packers. It marked one of the most significant victories in the long-running rivalry. After falling behind early, the Bears slowly battled back, scoring 25 points in the final quarter to seal the game transforming Soldier Field into a parade for the Chicago.

Caleb Williams speaks post-game after securing the Bears playoff berth.

The win carries extra weight for the city. Chicago has endured years of dominance by the Green Bay Packers, especially in high-stakes matchups, and Saturday's result offered a moment of relief and pride for fans who have waited a long time for a postseason breakthrough.

The game was filled with excitement:

  • Chicago stored 25 points in the fourth quarter.
  • First postseason victory over Green Bay in decades.
  • Chicago overcame an early deficit with key defensive stops and a late touchdown.
  • It marks a potential turning point for the team as they advance to the divisional round of the playoffs.

With the comeback, the Bears earned more than a spot in the next round. They secured a milestone win over their biggest rival and gave the city a reason to believe again. As Chicago moves forward in the playoffs, Saturday's performance will stand as one of the franchise's most meaningful moments in recent years.
